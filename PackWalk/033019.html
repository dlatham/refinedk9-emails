WHAT IS A PACK WALK?


Simply put, a pack walk is a group of dogs moving together in a migratory manner. These walks are not designed to facilitate any type of leash greetings or social interactions amongst dogs. Rather this walk is geared toward calm, relaxed, and structured exposure to other dogs in close proximity moving as a pack. Because dogs act differently during migration, this is a great opportunity to safely expose your dog to new outdoor areas, while improving your handling skills and increasing your dog’s tolerance of other dogs. Our extended pack walk will include you, and nine other owners and their dogs on structured walk or hike in areas with a leash law that is enforced. These pack walks are a great opportunity to get outdoors and continue working with your trainer in a smaller and more intimate setting.


WHAT MAKES THIS CLASS UNIQUE?


Unlike our LRA class, you will be working together as a single unit. You will practice passing each other but you will not be asking for more than simple obedience from your dog. The idea is to expose your dog to a new environment alongside other dogs without the pressure of interactions and advanced obedience. This helps you accomplish “dog neutrality” which is an important part of walking your dog out in the world. The more you expose your dog to other dogs in a structured and neutral environment, the more tolerant your dog becomes of others while remaining engaged and focused on you as the dog’s handler.


WHAT SHOULD I BRING TO THE PACK WALK?


Please bring a hungry dog! Skip feeding your dog’s normal breakfast, and bring their kibble along with some higher value food rewards so they can work during the walk. Don’t forget your treat pouch, clicker if you use one, leash and collar you are currently training with, (whatever walking equipment you have used with your trainer, prong, e-collar etc.), poop bags, and some water and a small dog water bowl if you’d like to be able to offer water to your dog along the way. Please wear comfortable walking shoes. The path is mostly flat, but avoid flip flops, heels, etc. We are looking forward to seeing you for our first east bay pack walk! Please let us know if you have any further questions. Feel free to arrive a few minutes early with your dog if you’d like to get them out and walk around for a moment before the walk begins.
